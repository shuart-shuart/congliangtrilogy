仨兰

Miao Chun 妙春was born in the 1880s, she was adopted by a childless doctor couple after she was abandoned. Her name alludes to the praise for doctors, 妙手回春 (magical hands that can bring the dying back to life). She served as a nurse during the First Sino-Japanese war and witnessed the atrocities of the Japanese. She carries a huge baggage towards the Japanese and Imperialist powers due to the damage and violence they have inflicted upon her homeland. Miao Chun lost her family during the war and departed to the Southern Seas with the inheritance they left her, eventually finding employment in Singapore as a nurse and settling in Chinatown. Miao Chun was killed in 1945 during the occupation due to her resistance towards the Japanese.

She has a heart of gold and is always eager to help others as she genuinely believed any help offered could save a life. Miao Chun was a key driving force for the club's conception, her expertise in medicine and sexual health was also key to the club's outreach and empowerment efforts. Her tenderhearted character was primed by having witness wars and deaths in her service, this equipped her with the tenacity and shrewdness needed to lead the club. She also learnt about poison from a pusher she met onboard the junk to Singapore. This came in helpful for the guerilla efforts she and Ya Lan championed during the war and Japanese Occupation. She also conceived a harmless medicine that cheated the money off prostitution clients to raise funds for the Chinese war efforts.
