仨兰

Born in 1904, Ya Lan 雅兰 was a prostitute and elusive about her past. Rumor has it that she was from a rich Hokkien family before the pirates took her and sold her to Singapore when a huge fire broke out in her hometown. Her name means elegant orchid and she possess the qualities of the flower, in fact she was almost comparable to the great courtesans of Late Ming Dynasty. She was a confident and smart lady, who was trained in the Four Arts of music, chess, calligraphy, and painting. Perhaps this was what gave her the last bit of dignity and determination to change the state of things. Despite her fierce independence and spirit, she fell prey to her pimp and was kept indebted due to her opium addiction.

In 1924, she was pregnant with Sheng Lan, refusing to let the young girl fall into the same fate, she went to the KK Hospital. At her insistence, Sheng Lan was kept in a room in the hospital and she developed a friendship with the nurse, Miao Chun. Later, Ya Lan, Miao Chun, and Ah Jiao formed the Cong Liang Club and was instrumental in empowering women both in peace and war times. The high social exposure from Ya Lan's prostitution work was beneficial, and she was often able to come up with interesting solutions that actualized the trio's visions. She was also a cultural connoisseur and tried to pass on the arts and women's craft she was so skilled in. She made the first emblem of the club, a special pair of earrings.

Ya Lan's venomous distaste for men escalated further during the war. She hated the Japanese soldiers for how they treated her fellow women and Chinese. This drove her to come up with insidious plans to attack them and help the comfort women as much as possible. Around 1945, a comfort woman ousted her, and she was taken away and allegedly tortured to death by the Japanese. Just like that, a legendary woman came and went, unknown and unrecorded.
